Robert Welsch

Scenario:

As the school's vice-president, Bobby has a week to research new, innovative ideas to keep the children engaged in class.

  • Expects to find an overwhelming amount of options
  • Expects to find interactive material to engage his students
  • Expects a certain amount of restraint from the higher ups, therefore needs a lot of information to support his final choice

Stages

Doing

Thinking

Saying

Awareness

Begins by contacting his network, before diving into a deep google search
"What can I find to engage our students?"
"I need to narrow down my options."

Consideration

Going back and forth between websites and SoMe of companies with focus on learning and primary school as keywords.
"Just because not enough studies have been made on this, doesn't mean this can't be a helpful tool."
"Big interactive screens where students can play and learn together - imagine that!"

Decision

Preparing notes on all of the new information and presenting the idea to the headmaster.
"The website didn't give me much, but a call with the CEO gave me all the information I needed!"
"I have made my decision."

Post-experience

Following WECO PLAY's SoMe. Overseeing the students hanging over the big screens.
"I can't believe they're actually willingly learning math."
"Mission accomplished."

Insights

  • WECO's website is straightforward, but lacks a pathos touch
  • Unnecessary amount of subpages

Internal ownership

  • The website lacks information
  • WECO's visual identity needs rearranging
  • UI design could use some improvement
